What is Overtime?

Overtime is the extra time you work beyond your regular working hours. In the U.S., the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) requires most employers to pay non-exempt employees at least 1.5 times their regular pay rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Under the 2026 No Tax on Overtime provision, the overtime premium may be excluded from federal income tax.

The formula used in this calculator is:

Overtime Laws in the USA

According to the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), non-exempt employees must be paid at least 1.5 times their regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. The 2026 overtime tax exemption may also allow the premium portion to be excluded from federal income tax.

Important Notes

  • •Overtime laws vary by state and country. Please check local labor laws for compliance.
  • •The 2026 overtime tax exemption covers the premium portion only — the base 1× rate remains taxable.
  • •FICA taxes (Social Security + Medicare) still apply to all overtime wages regardless of the exemption.
  • •Phase-out thresholds: $150,000 (Single), $300,000 (MFJ). High earners receive reduced exemption benefit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is overtime pay?

Overtime pay is compensation for hours worked beyond 40 per week. The FLSA requires at least 1.5× the regular rate for those extra hours.

What is the No Tax on Overtime rule?

The 2026 proposed exemption allows the overtime premium (extra above 1× rate) to be excluded from federal income tax, reducing your tax burden significantly.

What is MAGI?

MAGI (Modified Adjusted Gross Income) is your gross income minus certain deductions. Enter your income before overtime pay is added for accurate results.

Is overtime pay taxable?

Under the 2026 exemption, the overtime premium is federally tax-exempt. However, FICA taxes (6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare) still apply to all overtime wages.

What is the standard overtime rate?

The FLSA standard is 1.5× (time and a half) for hours over 40/week. Some employers offer 2× (double time) for holidays or weekends.

Does the phase-out affect savings?

Yes. Earners above $150k (Single) or $300k (MFJ) see a proportionally reduced exemption. The calculator adjusts savings automatically based on your MAGI.

What is the maximum overtime hours allowed?

There is no federal maximum on overtime hours — employees can work as many hours as agreed, as long as they receive proper overtime compensation.

How is overtime calculated for part-time?

Part-time employees earn overtime when they work more than 40 hours in a workweek, just like full-time workers. The 40-hour threshold applies to all non-exempt employees.
